2.Honey App

A masters student was doing their thesis on the blockchain and as part of it contracted me to create a web-based app prototype which interfaced with an IBM blockchain API they had made. The purpose of the project is to assist in Quality Assurance throughout the entire supply chain of New Zealand produced honey - from beekeepers to overseas shops. With some assistance communicating with API, I managed to deliver enough of a good looking prototype by the thesis presentation deadline that the professors and ultimately the customer was very happy. This was a challenging project because of the short timeframe and poorly communicated requirements (and unfinished API). This took a lot of initiative and running wireframes by the customer before actually implementing any pages.

Hives and Honey Extraction pages:
Hives page: See an overview of hives in your bee apiary.
Honey Extraction page: Google maps was used in the prototype to physically locate hives. A custom search field is overlaid on top of the map to assist in locating your extraction area.

The testing and storage pages:
The Testing Page; Lab samples are tested and logged in detail against the original honey batches. The list items can expand to include a form which can be filled out and submitted in-place (AJAX Post).
The Storage Page; A number of custom web-based controls were created, including these list items, some with custom styled check boxes.

Packing And Shipping Pages
Packing is where raw honey is packed into jars and boxes. Honey is carefully weighed and recorded along with the test results of the batch. All information is signed together on the IBM blockchain backend to prove each successive part of the supply chain.
Shipping Page:From storage in a warehouse, orders are shipped to stores internationally. The blockchain powering the app allows any jar of honey to be traced back to their test results and manuka source.
